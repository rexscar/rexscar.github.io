Reasons for policy

The purpose of this policy is to define the basis upon which functional credit authority is delegated down to committees and individuals employed in the Risk function of Standard Bank Corporate and Investment Banking (“CIB”) globally.

Related policies

 

This policy is to be read in conjunction with all other relevant Bank policies in force from time to time relating to the specific division, country, legal entity and business. 

The Group Credit Standard takes precedence over this policy and in the event of any conflict between this policy and the Group Credit Standard; the Group Credit Standard will prevail.

 

Applicability

 

This policy applies to all CIB functions globally.

Sponsor: Chief Risk Officer (“CRO”), CIB

Owner: Head: Corporate and Investment Banking Credit Risk

Approval:  Group Risk Oversight Committee

All those within CIB with responsibility for originating, structuring, recommending, and authorising credit risk.

2. Objectives of Policy

 

 

2.               The objectives of this Delegated Authority (“DA”) policy are:

2.1        To facilitate and enable CIB to operate as a single integrated business, irrespective of the legal entities into which risk assets may be booked such that the Standard Bank Group (excluding Liberty Holdings) always has one consistent credit view on a client;

2.2        To extend DA to individual Risk Officers on the basis of being personal to the holder in order to increase decision making accountability; 

2.3        As far as possible, to streamline the credit approval process in order to increase efficiency and effectiveness of the credit approval process; 

2.4        To optimally distribute the sanctioning workload appropriately across CIB between a cadre of experienced Risk Officers, taking cognisance of the quantum and riskiness of specific risk assets, the Bank’s risk appetite, the Bank’s capital base and business volumes;   

2.5        To provide for transparent oversight of the decision making of individual holders of DA in order to ensure that the quality of decision making remains consistent and within the risk appetite of the Bank and that the Group Credit Standard is being consistently applied;

2.6        To ensure that any limit to a counterparty or economic group1 is approved at the appropriate level of DA and such limits receives the appropriate degree of attention. The higher the limits and/or the higher the risk profile of the counterparty or economic group, the higher the DA level or approval. Where such approvals are deemed material to the

Bank’s performance, they shall be reported to CIB Credit Governance Committee at the discretion of the relevant DA authority; 

2.7        To enshrine the ‘four-eyes2’ principle for all credit approvals, while ensuring excesses are approved at appropriate DA level. 

2.8        To ensure that the credit view is applied consistently for counterparties or economic groups by ensuring that ownership of any counterparty or economic group resides with a single credit risk team / officer (in line with the Group Credit Manager responsibilities guidelines) regardless of the jurisdictions involved. 

 

 

1                      Economic Group: As defined in the CIB Credit policy, where two or more natural or legal persons who are interlinked and deemed to pose a “single risk”, either by way of control or significant economic interdependence.

(refer to the CIB Credit policy for comprehensive definition)

2                      4-eye principle is defined as DA holder acting in conjunction with another DA holder. In cases where Credit has agreed that Front-Office teams originate credit applications, and approved by the relevant DA holder, it is deemed that the ‘4eyes’ requirement has been complied with. Should there be a request for any other business unit or legal entity to adopt this business model, approval from the CIB Head, Credit Risk would have to be obtained

3.  Functional vs. Legal Entity Credit Approval

 

3.1 This policy distinguishes between Functional Credit approval and Legal Entity Credit approval.  

3.1.1 “Functional Credit Approval” is an assessment of the creditworthiness of a particular credit proposal applying the principles enshrined in the Group Credit Risk Governance Standard and based on the risk assessment of the individual counterparty or economic group. This should take into account, inter alia, industry risk, the country or countries of risk as well as specific product or structural issues and their appropriateness in line with credit policies across all legal entities exposed to that counterparty or economic group. However, this functional approval will not necessarily take into consideration any specific issues which may be relevant to the specific legal entity within Standard Bank Group where any asset thus approved is expected to be booked, such as regulatory or compliance provisions specific to the legal entity as outlined in clause 3.1.2.

3.1.2 “Legal Entity[1] Credit Approval” considers any issues which may arise with respect to the specific legal entity within Standard Bank Group where an asset is expected to be booked.  This would include, inter alia, specific legal entity matters related to booking the asset on the specific legal entity’s balance sheet (e.g. liquidity, the requirement for the Board support of the specific legal entity), exchange control, single obligor limit constraints, general regulatory concerns, common directorship conflicts, legal entity concentrations, and local accountability to and responsibility of legal entity stakeholders to ensure that the risk reward dynamics for that legal entity remain appropriate and accepted.

3.2 This policy deals with Functional Credit approval authority in the first instance. Policies dealing with “Legal Entity Credit Approval” authority may be required to be procured within the specific Legal Entity on whose balance sheet the credit exposure will reside as detailed above. To the extent possible and in the interests of efficiency, Legal Entities should aim to adopt[2] this Policy as their Legal Entity Credit Approval Policy, such that  

-          all Delegated Authority holders mandated in terms of this policy, and employed by the Legal Entity concerned, may be regarded as dispensing both Legal Entity and Functional Credit approval simultaneously, when exercising their approval mandates in respect of business to be booked on the balance sheet of that Legal Entity, and 

-          where Functional Credit approval is required for exposures exceeding the mandate of the highest Delegated Authority holder domiciled in the Legal Entity concerned, the express recommendation of that holder and, as applicable, the Credit Risk

 

Management Committee or Credit Committee of that Legal Entity, shall constitute the Legal Entity Credit Approval. 

 

 

4.

Delegated Authority Grid

 

4.1

 

DA in terms of this policy is conferred by Standard Bank Group Risk Oversight Committee to CIB Credit Governance Committee, CIB Credit Committee and to

individual Risk Officers in accordance with the DA Grid (Annexure 1) 

4.2

The DA Grid is an integral component of the DA approval process as it displays the maximum credit limits that can be approved by a DA level[3] for a single counterparty or economic group based on either the counterparty’s individual internal credit rating, or on a risk weighted average based credit rating for the economic group. Given that Standard Bank’s core capital is in ZAR, the DA Grid for CIB is expressed in ZAR. 

4.3

 

 

Given the extent of the Bank’s International and African operations, limits can also be applied for in USD. The guiding principle is that a Risk Officer should use the currency most applicable to the exposure against the underlying counterparty or economic group and CIB Credit functions in each country should consistently adopt either USD or ZAR limits. Where USD is used for total limits applied for, the prescribed exchange rate[4]should be used to convert USD limits to ZAR for DA calculation purposes. 

5.

 

Awarding of Delegated Authority

 

 

5.1        A holder of a DA in terms of this policy has the authority to take credit decisions within this authority which are binding from a functional credit perspective, regardless of the Standard Bank Group legal entity within which the deal is to be booked. 

5.2        DA is personal to the holder and cannot be sub-delegated except as specifically approved by the relevant Governance Authority (GROC) or as provided for in par 8.6 of this policy, nor does it attach to the rank or position held by the individual to which it has

 

been awarded.  Given their committee structure and mandates, DA 1 to DA 3 has the ability to delegate specific approval authority to a lower-level DA holder. 

5.3        Risk Officers will be advised in writing of the level of DA accorded to them in terms of this policy.

5.4        DA remains valid until the earlier of:

5.4.1       The withdrawal in writing by the awarding authority in terms of this policy; or 

5.4.2       Should the individual no longer be employed in a relevant CIB Risk Function of any legal entity within the Standard Bank Group. 

5.5         DA recommendations are to be based upon the qualification and experience of the individual being nominated and must be made in writing and supported by at least one Risk officer holding a DA4 authority. When considering recommendations reference should be made to the “DA Motivation Guideline”

5.6         Notwithstanding the decision-making authority afforded any holder of a DA, at the sole discretion of that DA holder and request may be referred to a higher DA level for consideration.

 

 

6. Levels of Delegated Authority

 

 

6.1 There are 10 DA levels

6.2 DA 1 – CIB Credit Governance Committee

6.2.1 This is the ultimate credit approving authority for CIB and can only be awarded by

Standard Bank Group Risk Oversight Committee to CIB Credit Governance Committee and the Intragroup Exposure Committee which have been constituted, inter alia, for the purpose of exercising that authority. The primary restriction on these committees’ authority is the Regulatory Credit Approval requirements specific to each legal entity within the Standard Bank Group.

6.2.2 In principle however the main focus of this committee with respect to DA is to ensure that DA is exercised in a consistent manner, with due consideration for the Standard Bank Group’s risk appetite. 

6.2.3 This responsibility is discharged by CIB Credit Governance Committee exercising the duties and responsibilities assigned to it in terms of its Mandate as amended from time to time.

6.2.4 A Standard Bank Group Non-Executive Director will be invited to form part of the CIB Credit Governance Committee for DA1 level credit decisions where such applications would not subsequently be tabled at the applicable Large Exposure (LE) Committee. South Africa Large Exposure “SALE” committee for SBSA Limits and SBG Large Exposure committee for Total Group Limits.    

6.3  DA 2 – CIB Credit Committee, Structured Transactions Committee (“STC”) and Intragroup Exposure Committee 

6.3.1 Approval authority under this policy is as per the DA Grids in Annexure 1.

6.3.2 CIB Credit Committee, STC and Intragroup Exposure Committee will operate in terms of their Mandates which are to be approved and reviewed at least biennially by CIB Credit Governance Committee.

6.3.3    All Standard Bank Group subsidiary approvals are to be referred to the Intragroup Exposure Committee. Note: All approvals below DA 2 level should be handled in line with the DA grid in Annexure 1.

         6.4     DA 3 – CIB Credit Panel

6.4.1 The DA level allocated to any two senior Risk Officers both holding personal DA 4 mandates and acting together on a consensus basis. 

 

         6.5     DA  4

6.5.1 In principle this is the highest level of DA accorded to any individual by CIB Governance Committee.6.5.2 DA 4 holders act in conjunction with another DA holder and a Business Sponsor. 

         6.6     DA Levels 5, 6, 7 and 8  

6.6.1  DA levels 5 to 8 are awarded to specific individuals by the Head, CIB Risk, Corporate Credit and the Head, Portfolio Management and Risk Reporting or any other individual or forum to which this power has been delegated to.

6.6.2  These DA holders are authorized to exercise their approval authority acting in conjunction with another DA holder (4-eye principle) and a Business Sponsor (for their support).  

           

         6.7     Level 9 DA 

6.7.1 DA 9 is restricted to the management of excesses and to providing recommendations for credit proposals to be supported and approved by a more senior individual DA holder. 

6.7.2 The authority to grant DA level 9 is delegated to individual risk officers holding DA4 approval authority.

         6.8     DA 10 Business Support and Resolution Working Groups  

6.8.1 When a credit exposure is placed on the Watchlist or becomes a Non- Performing Loan, Business Support and Resolution are to work closely with the existing client/deal team in order to identify the cause of the concerns and establish a strategy for rehabilitating the client/recovery.

6.8.2 As part of that process it may be necessary to agree to additional funds being advanced. 

6.8.3 For the purpose of approving new monies for names on the Watchlist, a specific DA comprised of representatives of Business Support and Resolution, Credit and Business is required. Decisions will be based on consensus of the three votes; Business Support and Resolution, represented by a Business Support and Resolution Executive, will have one vote. Credit represented by an individual with DA 4 authority will have one vote. Business represented by the Head of the affected Business line will have one vote. The aforementioned representatives may elect to escalate the credit decision to a DA2 level as they deem necessary.

6.8.4 Once a name is on Watchlist, all approvals for new monies must be under this DA 10 mandate or, if exceeding this DA 10 mandate, DA 1 or DA 2. 

6.8.5 For names identified as non-performing, a special DA for new monies is extended on a 4 eyes principle, as outlined in clause 6.8.3, basis as follows:

-          Global Head of Business Support and Resolution: up to USD10 million

-          Business Support and Resolution Executives: up to USD5 million 

-          Business Support and Resolution Senior Managers: up to USD2.5 million 

6.8.6 Business Support and Resolution Executives have authority to deal with all other credit aspects in relation Non-Performing names including agreeing repayment moratoriums, formal facility agreements or any other form of facility restructure or renegotiation.

6.8.7  DA 10 Working Group meetings are to be conducted and recorded as though they are Credit Committee meetings.

           

6.9  Additional requirement for Non-Executive Director

A Standard Bank Group Non-Executive Director (“NED”) will be invited to participate in:

6.9.1   All DA2 and lower CIB Credit decisions (including STC risk decisions) considering approval for credit limits in excess of USD100m for Economic Groups or individual borrowers where Country of Risk is designated as Outside of South Africa.

 It should however be noted that the requirement for a NED to be invited won’t apply under the following circumstances:

6.9.1.1 Credit decision relating to a regulated bank entity (Non-bank financial institutions are excluded from this exception); 

6.9.1.2. Credit decision relating to Internally rated investment grade Economic Groups or individual borrowers whose Country of Risk is also an internally rated investment grade country;

6.9.1.3   Annual reviews of Economic Groups or individual borrowers which present no material changes, in risk profile and limit amount from the previous approval, as determined by a CIB Credit Risk DA 4 mandate holder;

6.9.1.4   Credit approvals which need to be tabled at SALE or SBG Large Exposure Committee;

6.9.1.5 Credit decision where the limits sought excluding non-discretionary / run the bank limits do not exceed USD100m.

6.9.2. Should a NED not be available to participate in the respective CIB Credit decision meeting, then the meeting will still proceed and credit decisions taken in their absence will be binding under the respective DA voting mandate.

           

 

7. Delegated Authority Exemptions 

 

7.1 Cash Backed Trade Finance Products

7.1.1  Any Risk Officer holding DA level 7 or more senior, while adhering to the four eyes principle, has the additional authority to approve cash backed trade finance facilities, for an eligible applicant and product, up to a maximum of 3 times their DA in terms of the DA Grid, provided the transaction meets all the following conditions:

-                  cash collateral held is the primary source of settlement for obligations;

-                  currency of the limit / exposure is the same as the currency of the collateral;

-                  cash collateral is pledged in a Standard Bank / Stanbic Bank account (or cash is held in a cash margin account in a Standard Bank / Stanbic Bank account) a positive legal opinion is in place, and confirmation received from TPS that pledge indicator has been loaded onto the relevant deposit account; -          cash balance is sufficient to cover facilities applied for in full;

-                  tenor of the facility is within 12 months.

  To the extent that a jurisdiction's laws may permit the lodgement of cash with the bank as an alternative to pledging a deposit account in the client’s name, confirmation of the bank’s irrevocable recourse to such cash lodged must be supported by a blanket legal opinion held for the relevant jurisdiction, or alternatively must be confirmed by a lawyer qualified in that jurisdiction. In providing the aforementioned confirmation, the lawyer must consider the transaction’s specific details and circumstances.

7.1.2   In the absence of financial statements being received to determine the counterparty risk grade, counterparties will be allocated a deemed risk grade at a level equivalent to the applicable country’s Foreign Currency rating but capped at no better than SB18 without the standard rating due diligence being applied. In order to apply this rating dispensation in conjunction with par 7.1.1, the following requirements would have to be met:

-                  Internal legal opinion to be obtained confirming that insolvency laws applicable to the relevant jurisdiction will not affect the bank’s right to cash held as collateral.

-                  Should the above legal opinion be unfavourable; an auditor’s confirmation will have to be obtained to confirm solvency of the borrower.

If the above requirements are not clearly met, the 3x DA concession allowed as per paragraph 7.1.1 would not apply, and the DA applicable would be as per the standard DA Matrix.  

 

7.1.3 For the avoidance of doubt it should be noted that the exemption as per par 7.1.2 will only apply to the extent that facilities are cash covered. Should there be a mix between cash covered and non-cash covered facilities, a weighted DA calculation will be required as noncash covered facilities are excluded from the scope of this exemption. This weighting may be calculated by dividing the cash-covered component of the facilities by three, adding the quotient to the non-cash covered component of the facilities, and the resulting total is the reference figure for determining DA level according to the grid.    

 

7.2 Reliance or Financing of Physical Commodities 

7.2.1  All credit exposures and limits, regardless of amount, which place reliance on physical commodity collateral for regulatory capital relief, or where the credit limits applied for would change in the absence of reliance on the physical commodity collateral, are required to meet the Minimum Requirements for Reliance on Physical Commodity Collateral as described in Annexure 5.

7.2.2  Where any of the Minimum Requirements in Annexure 5 are not met or cannot be met for any reason, approval is required from the STC. 

7.2.3  The approval will be granted in terms of the current STC mandate, with the specific requirement of the designated Credit Risk Voting Member, as defined in the STC mandate, being part of the STC quorum and if required a NED (refer section 6.9).   

 

 

8. Scope of Delegated Authority

 

 

8.1 With the exception of DA 10, the basis for determining the level of delegated authority required for approval, shall be based on this DA Framework using the total limits to the individual counterparty or an economic group. Where applicable, economic groups as defined in the CIB Credit Policy are to be treated as a single counterparty for DA purposes, except in the cases specified in 8.2 below.  

8.2   DA levels for request for facilities are determined based on the aggregate of risk-weighted limits sought for the economic group.  Risk-weighted limits are generally regarded as the notional amount for primary facilities and the PFE amount for trading facilities, adjusted for netting where enforceable and subject to the necessary regulatory recognition.   

  8.2.1 The required DA level applicable for a credit approval sought for a counterparty within an economic group, (refer to example B under Annexure 3) with due reference to the Counterparty Concentration Approval Policy, will be the most senior DA indicated on the Grid, based on 

i)    the aggregate limit weighted average rating for the group, with reference to the total Bank limits to the economic group, or 

ii)  the individual risk grade of a counterparty within the economic group requesting a review or an increase in facilities limits. 

8.2.2  In cases where there are pre-existing or newly proposed equity limits approved by the Equity Risk Committee for an economic group, the following rules would apply in addition to the principles contained in paragraph 8.2.1:

8.2.2.1 The DA level applicable to the credit limits would be as per the DA matrix excluding equity limits approved by Equity Risk Committee, but no lower than a DA 4. 

8.2.2.2  The approving DA should obtain confirmation from the Equity Risk Team that limits recorded against equity risk have been approved at the required level. 

8.2.2.3  Paragraph 8.2.2.1 should not detract from any disclosure requirements in terms of total bank exposure to an economic group, and all limits (whether debt or equity in nature) should be disclosed in all credit applications. 

8.3 In order to deal with relatively small changes to existing facilities, any holder of a DA mandate may approve an increase in the facilities of a counterparty or economic Group, of not more than the lower of:

8.3.1 10% of the total limits originally approved by the original approving DA; or 

8.3.2 an individual’s mandated Excesses Limit Authority as per the DA Grid.

8.4    The dispensation described in 8.3 must be applied on a cumulative basis.  That is, several small increases over a defined period of time, not exceeding 12 months, being the intervening period between annual reviews, should be assessed accordingly, and where they collectively breach the 10% threshold, the DA level applicable to the total limits for the counterparty or economic group will apply (Refer to Annexure 3 for an illustrative example).  

  8.5    For any individual counterparty accorded multiple facilities, the need to reallocate limits for one type of facility to another type often arises. For any counterparty, there is no objection to such reallocations being approved by any DA holder, provided the reallocation entails no increase in the credit risk attaching to the counterparty at hand, and that the legal agreements in place would still provide the same protection to the bank before and after the reallocation. The following conditions should be met in respect of such reallocation approvals:

8.5.1  Affected limits are reallocated between facilities accorded to the same           counterparty, or in the case of a group facility sub-allocated to multiple           counterparties within the group, strictly within the ambit of cross guarantee recourse covering all such counterparties, and 

8.5.2 Facilities reallocated are of equal tenor, or from a longer to a shorter tenor, and 

8.5.3 Unsecured facilities may be reallocated to secured facilities, and secured facilities may be reallocated to other secured facilities with the same type of collateral, yielding equivalent or greater collateral value, and 

8.5.4 Reallocations are within the same measure (Primary to Primary, Pre-Settlement  to Pre-Settlement, Settlement to Settlement, And Gross to Gross). Primary Limits may be switched into Pre-Settlement, but not the other way round (refer to paragraph 8.5.7 and 8.5.8 for specific exceptions). As a matter of consistency in measure, Settlement and Gross measure limits cannot be reallocated between each other, or to other measure types, and 

8.5.5 Reallocation from an on demand primary facility such as GSTBF to Performance guarantees or sight or usance Letters of Credit may be accommodated, provided the tenor applicable to the instrument issued is not more than 12 months (Standby Letters of credit and lending guarantees are specifically excluded from this dispensation, and are regarded as presenting a net increase in risk), and 

8.5.6 The reallocation does not entail the use of any limits approved as part of a           structured package whose components are mutually interdependent.

 

If the proposed reallocation does not clearly meet all of the above conditions, it is deemed to represent a net increase in risk and must be referred to the relevant DA level as outlined in paragraph 8.3.  

8.5.7   Specific exceptions to paragraph 8.5 are as follows: 

8.5.7.1 Reallocation between different types of sovereign non-discretionary runthe-bank limits, which might result in a net increase in risk. Switches of this nature would have to be approved by at least a DA 4;

8.5.7.2 Reallocation from Pre-Settlement to Primary limits is allowed if the requirements as per paragraphs 8.5.1, 8.5.2 and 8.5.3 have been met. Such reallocation would however have to be approved by the original sanctioning authority, or by a DA 4 where the original sanctioning authority applicable to the economic group falls within DA 1 to DA 3. 

8.5.8   For the avoidance of doubt, limits approved for amortising facilities are reduced as and when scheduled repayments fall due. It follows that reduction of exposure and consequently reduction of limits arising out of amortisation of such previously approved limits is not to be regarded as being approved credit appetite available for reallocation to other facilities unless specific approval has been granted to reallocate such amortized portion into Headroom limits.

It is also possible for the original approving DA to support and approve that the amortised portion of a facility can be made available for re-allocation/ re-draw, subject to there being no overall change in risk profile.   

Other than for group facilities accorded to multiple borrowers as envisaged in

8.5.1, a reallocation of approved facilities between different counterparties within

an economic group require consideration of multiple risks and mitigation factors. Such reallocations do not fall within the above dispensation and should be referred to the original approving DA level, or DA4, whichever is the lower.  

 

8.5.9   Where any of the economic grouping principles contained in par. 6.d of the CIB Credit Policy are triggered for entities not previously grouped for DA purposes and a view is presented to keep such economically grouped entities separate for DA purposes, an application setting out the basis of the interdependence and rationale/motivation for de-grouping should be tabled at the higher DA level determined with reference to the total combined limits of all such entities, as if they were economically grouped. If the motivation is agreed to by such DA level, subsequent reviews may be tabled at lower relevant DA levels determined with reference to the respective facility levels of each entity.

           In the event of an existing economic group being unbundled or a newly identified economic group being instated, the DA applicable to the entire group would apply.

8.5.10 Approval of any foreign currency loans should be assessed in conjunction with the Foreign Currency Loan – Credit Guidelines. 

8.6  In determining the delegated authority applicable to an annual review on a rotational[5] basis, the delegated authority could be lower than the original approving delegated authority as per paragraph 8.6.1 for a maximum of two subsequent annual reviews, if all the requirements stipulated in Par 8.6.2 have been met.

8.6.1  Delegated Authority applicable to a qualifying rotational annual review would be as follows: 

                               8.6.1.1  DA 2 annual reviews are delegated to a DA 3 

8.6.1.2   DA 3 annual reviews are delegated to a DA 4

                               8.6.1.3   DA 4 annual reviews are delegated to a DA 5

8.6.1.4   DA 5 annual reviews are delegated to a DA 6

8.6.2  In order to apply the rotational review delegation allowed in terms of Par 8.6.1, all of the following requirements would have to be met: 

8.6.2.1  No deterioration in the weighted risk grade of the economic group since the risk grade approved by the relevant sanctioning authority at the time of the previous annual credit review. 

  Should there be a deterioration in a risk grade which remains investment grade post such deterioration, the review would still qualify for delegation

 

in terms of paragraph 5.8.1 if the number of notches move in the risk grade does not exceed 2 notches; and

                               8.6.2.2   No financial covenant breaches; and 

                        8.6.2.3   No adverse changes in terms of structural or legal subordination; and

8.6.2.4   No adverse changes in terms of our collateral or priority claims; and

8.6.2.5  Total level of facilities applied for remains within 10% of total facilities approved by the original delegated authority as per the DA Matrix; and

8.6.2.6  None of the borrowers have been on Watchlist or Close Monitoring in the past 6 months; and

8.6.2.7  PRMC Credit appetite is either:

 “Add Subject To”; or

 “Hold” (subject to no increase in limits). 

8.6.2.8 Total limits are within Counterparty Concentration Appetite limits

 

8.7 The following risk measures (8.7.1 – 8.7.4) are non-additive when establishing a counterparty or economic group limit, and are additional to a Risk Officer’s risk-weighted DA.

8.7.1 Settlement Risk Limits:  In addition to the DA granted for risk-weighted limits, DA holders may exercise their authority to approve aggregate same-day settlement risk limits as follows: 

8.7.1.1 Notional limit increases at account level, which includes non-fated electronic banking limits, may be approved on the following basis:

a)  Where a signed facility’s letter is held, or where a notional limit indemnity has been signed:

i)   DA8 and more senior levels can approve notional limits up to a maximum amount equal to the GSTBF /working capital facility limit approved.

ii)  DA6 and or more senior levels can approve notional limits up to an amount equal to their approval mandate as per the DA matrix approval column.

iii) Any notional limits in excess of the amount specified in par 8.7.1.1 (a) (ii) would require DA4 approval

b)  Where there is no legal protection held as defined in par 8.7.1.1(a)

i)   DA6 and more senior levels can approve up to R10 million (or USD equivalent using the prescribed exchange rate[6]).

ii)  Any notional limits in excess of the amount specified in par

8.7.1.1 (b) (i) would require DA4 approval

8.7.1.2 Temporary lifting of fating, where the amount can be capped by the   system, may be approved in line with par 8.7.1.1

8.7.1.3 Permanent lifting of fating, or where there is a temporary lifting with no capped amount specified, as well as any other non-fated limits, must be approved at DA4 level.  

8.7.1.4 Trading settlement limit increases may be approved by DA8 or more senior levels, up to 7 times their approval authority in terms of the DA matrix approval column  

8.7.1.5 Any other intraday limit or fated limit increases not covered above may be approved in line with par 8.7.1.1

 

8.7.2 Financial Institutions, consisting of both Banks and non-Bank Financial

Institutions:

8.7.2.1 Where there is no GSTBF, or Working Capital facility and as both notional overdraft limits and non-fated Business On-line facilities are considered intra-day (settlement type) facilities (which definition and reference can be obtained from the Notional Overdraft and Lifting of fating procedure document) these limits may be approved by DA8 or more senior levels, up to 7 times their approval authority in terms of the DA matrix approval column (Paragraph 8.7.1.4)

8.7.2.2 Temporary lifting of fating, where the amount can be capped by the system, may be approved by DA8 or more senior levels, up to 7 times their approval authority in terms of the DA matrix approval column.

 

8.7.3 Gross Securities Financing Limits:  

8.7.3.1  In addition to the DA granted for risk-weighted limits, DA holders may exercise their authority to approve gross securities financing limits.  Gross limits are required for transactions where underlying assets referenced in securities financing transactions (or their synthetic equivalent) are not rated G1 or G2 (refer to Annexure 6 for definitions).

8.7.3.2 Securities Financing Limits need to comply with the requirements of the Securities Financing Policy and the Wrong Way Risk Policy. The maximum amount of total gross financing per customer / counterparty will be in line with

 

the Customer limit set in the Global Markets Client Finance Procedure Document. 

8.7.3.3 The determination of DA for gross securities financing limits for assets rated G3 – G5, will be subject to a separate DA calculation and based on an illiquid gross measure.  Any related pre-settlement risk generated by the transaction(s) will be additive to the calculation of primary, pre-settlement and Credit issuer limits for the counterparty and for the purposes of calculating appropriate DA levels. The highest sanctioning DA, based on the above two distinct DA calculations, will be required to approve any limit requests.

 

8.7.4 Gross Inventory Financing Limits:

Limits for repo of physical commodities are not permitted without approval of the CIB Chief Risk Officer.     

8.7.5 ‘Fully-Margined’ Pre-settlement Trading Products:  

Given the zero-credit limit nature of fully-margined pre-settlement facilities, any two holders of DA4 - DA8 holders have the authority to approve fully-margined trading facilities, up to a net notional value of USD100m.    

‘Fully Margined’ is to be interpreted as a pre-settlement risk transaction(s) whereby all initial margin (or Potential Future Exposure equivalent) and variation margin (or Mark to Market) credit risk exposure is fully collateralised, by cash or other liquid collateral acceptable to Credit (i.e. credit limits provided are zero). 

The following conditions will apply; 

i)         Relevant trading documentation must be executed and in place prior to trading.

ii)       Tenor is restricted to a maximum 12 months or the maximum clearing period for on-exchange transactions. iii) Any fully-margined counterparty with net notional exposure above USD100m will require DA3 approval authority[7].

For the avoidance of doubt, the requirement for DA3 approval for all net notional exposures above USD100m will also apply to margined base metal and precious metal trading counterparties documented under FOA, Terms of Business or Master Trading Agreements, where primary or pre-settlement credit limits are approved under DA authority.  In this scenario, the highest sanctioning DA, based on the two relevant DA calculations, will be required for approval.

 

         8.8    SB Intra-Group Limits / Exposures

Intra-Group Exposure Committee (‘IGEC’) delegated its approval of intra-group exposures to CIB DA2 credit committee level with special quorate requirements as per the Intra-Group Exposure Committee Mandate, up to the level of the delegated authority as determined by the GRCMC, which is currently USD1 billion. All intra-group exposures above this level of authority should be referred to GALCO who will refer to GRCMC for approval or referral where appropriate.

 

DA for this exposure category is different to the rest of the CIB portfolio and the quorum for DA2 relating to Intra-group exposures is shown below;

 

i)         The quorum for DA2 level approval (CIB Credit Committee) relating to IGEC approvals will comprise of the Head, CIB Risk, Corporate Credit or their alternate, Head FI Credit Risk CIB or his/her alternate, Head of Group Treasury & Capital Management Standard Bank or his/her alternate and the Head of Group Capital Management or their alternate. 

 

ii)        The delegated authority can choose to recommend the request to IGEC for approval should they be unable to reach a consensus.

 

iii)      IGEC furthermore grants full discretion to the Head, FI Credit Risk CIB to refer intragroup credit decisions directly to IGEC were considered appropriate.

 

8.9 Information omissions and covenant breaches

8.9.1  Information omissions, covenant breaches, or circumstances where covenant breaches have been identified must be escalated in line with Annexure 4.1, with an appropriate motivation jointly from the Primary Credit Manager and Global Credit Manager, to the relevant DA for the economic group to which the facility forms part, in order to decide either to condone or call a formal breach. 

8.9.2  Payment breaches need to be escalated immediately and BS&R should be notified accordingly, while financial covenant breaches should be escalated by no later than 5 working days of becoming aware of the breach. All other escalations need to take place within 10 working days of becoming aware of the omission and/or breach. 

8.10 Subsequent amendments to conditions of sanction  

8.10.1 There might be circumstances which require an amendment of or deviation from the original conditions of sanction. When such a request is assessed, it is important to establish the materiality of the change in order to determine the relevant DA approval required in line with Annexure 4.2. 

 

           

8.11 General

 

8.11.1  All bridges or facilities where the primary source of repayment is refinancing and or the realization of assets need the approval of at least a DA4 regardless of the size or risk grade. 

8.11.2 Any proposal requiring tenor of longer than 7 years needs to be approved by at least a DA 4, regardless of size, currency, or risk grade. This rule applies except with respect to:

8.11.2.1   Property Finance[8] deals where the maximum tenor applicable is 10 years and assets with longer dated final maturities but held for trading purposes and managed under a Market Risk framework. For the purposes of this policy, legally enforceable break clauses and additional termination events are deemed to curtail the tenor of the facility to the term of the break, or

8.11.2.2.  Project Finance transactions which have been in the operational phase for at least a year post construction, could be approved by the relevant DA level as per DA matrix, although not lower than a DA6 mandate holder.

8.11.3 Credit applications will require Business sign-off / support by Coverage and the relevant product line; TPS, Investment Banking and Global Markets as applicable. At its own discretion, Credit may require sign-off at a higher level.

8.11.4 All approvals are required to be documented (digitally using the Digital Credit Paper (DCP) platform where available), in the form of a credit application recommended by a credit officer who has personally undertaken the level of due diligence required in order to be satisfied that the credit paper adequately reflects the request and the credit risks involved.

8.11.5   Any approvals for names which have been added to the Watchlist would need to be determined in line with paragraph 6.8 as well as the CIB Business Support and Resolution Operating Mandate. 

  For all DA7 and DA8 level annual reviews of Watchlisted names, the minimum DA level which can jointly approve an annual review together with BS&R, is a DA6 (in line with the approval matrix outlined in the CIB Business Support and Resolution Operating Mandate).  

8.11.6 Where headroom limits are approved, it is important to include specific headroom allocation parameters, including but not limited to maximum tenor, DA level delegated to as well as any restrictions imposed such as facility type, currency, risk grade etc. It is also important to ensure that where an allocation from headroom results in a net increase in risk due to the allocation being outside of sanctioned headroom parameters, that the provisions of par 8.5 will apply. Cognisance should also be taken where the allocation of headroom would result in a deterioration in the overall weighted risk grade of the economic group, potentially resulting in a higher DA approval level and/or lower CCAP limit.   

 

 

9. Delegated Authority Oversight

 

 

9.1 Oversight of the exercise of DA is undertaken in various ways including but not limited to portfolio reporting, MIS statistics, Rapid Risk Reviews as well as hind sighting. Furthermore, independent external interventions conducted by Group Internal Audit as well as Group Risk (Credit Risk Review) provide an additional formal layer of oversight on the application of this policy.

ANNEXURE 3

       4.1     Information omissions and covenant breaches

Any Information omissions and covenant breaches, or circumstances where covenant breaches have been identified or are reasonably likely to occur, must be escalated, with an appropriate motivation jointly from the Primary Credit Manager and Global Credit Manager, to the relevant DA as prescribed below. 

For the purpose of determining the relevant Delegated Authority approval level required, information omissions and covenant breaches need to be categorised as one of the following:  i.) Information Breach

An information breach can be defined as the non-provision of information contractually required periodically by the bank to monitor the financial position or performance of a borrower. 

Should an information breach occur, approval would need to be obtained from the original sanctioning authority applicable to the economic group.  In the event where the applicable sanctioning authority is a DA1-DA4, approval would need to be obtained from a DA5, unless any borrower of that economic group has been added to Close Monitoring, which would then require DA4 approval. Where there is reason to believe that there might be a formal covenant breach which cannot be confirmed as a result of the omission of information, these circumstances need to be escalated to the original sanctioning authority at the earliest opportunity. 

 

 

ii.) Covenant Breach

A Covenant breach would occur when a financial covenant of undertaking included in a legal agreement has not been met been complied with. This could include inter alia ratio covenants, positive or negative undertakings.

Should a formal covenant breach occur, approval would need to be obtained from the original sanctioning authority applicable to the economic group.  

 

       4.2    Subsequent amendments to conditions of sanction

i.)   Material changes in conditions of sanction

Material changes can be defined as any change to a condition of sanction which would result in a material adverse change in risk to the Bank. 

While the below is not intended to be an exhaustive list of changes regarded to be material, the following are the most common examples of material changes:

a.)         Increase in the tenor of facilities applied for

b.)         Change in the borrower of record

c.)          Change in covenant levels

d.)         Change in the repayment profile of facility

e.)         Change in the ranking of facilities

These changes would have to be referred to the original sanctioning authority for approval given its nature. Where uncertainty exists around whether a specific change requested would be regarded as being material for groups which require DA1-DA3 approval, a DA4 would need to determine the level of materiality of the change, at the DA’s sole discretion, and would need to decide whether the proposed change to the conditions of sanction requires approval by the original sanctioning authority. In the event where an economic group requires Large Exposure approval, the Chief Credit Officer would need to determine whether changes requested warrants a resubmission to the committee.   

       

ii.)  Minor amendments to conditions of sanction

Minor changes can be defined as any change to a condition of sanction which could result in an adverse change in risk to the Bank, however the impact of the change is expected to be immaterial at the time. 

While the below is not intended to be an exhaustive list of minor changes, the following are the most common examples:

a.)         Change in thresholds such as cross-default, negative pledge thresholds or guarantor thresholds.

b.)         Change in frequency of information undertakings or days within which specific information needs to be provided to the bank 

These changes would have to be referred to the original sanctioning authority for approval given its nature, and where DA1-DA3 approval is required, notwithstanding par 5.7 of this policy, a DA4 would be mandated to approve such changes. 

iii.) Amendments to terms and conditions which have not been explicitly stated as conditions of sanction 

Amendments to terms and conditions which have not been explicitly stated as conditions of sanction are typically changes to legal agreements which have not been formally covered in the sanction.

While the below is not intended to be an exhaustive list of these changes, the following are the most common examples:

a.)         Nominal change in notice periods contained in facility letters

b.)         Setting of percentage applicable to wide fluctuations on GSTBF where sanction is silent on the required level. 

c.)          Nominal changes to remedy periods

 

These changes could be approved by any DA8 or higher mandate holder.

5.1 Minimum Requirements for Reliance on Physical Commodity Collateral

It is recommended that the following minimum requirements be met for reliance to be placed on Physical Commodity Collateral for CIB credit approval purposes. Where any of these minimum requirements are not met or cannot be met for any reason, approval is required from the STC: i.) Customer Limits

a)       Credit limits supported by reliance on physical collateral equal to or exceeding USD100 million (ratings SB1 to SB12 inclusive), USD75 million (ratings SB13 to SB18 inclusive) and USD25 million (ratings SB19 and below) to require STC approval

b)       Collateral documentation to be approved by Legal with original receipts signed by authorized signatories required unless otherwise approved by Legal

c)       Collateral valuation and haircuts to be approved by Market Risk ii.)      Inventory Location Limits

a)       Credit to approve a notional inventory limit for each location at which physical inventory is to be held, reflecting the risks of loss and frustration associated with the location

b)       Credit to specify the insurance cover required and Legal to approve the policy wording in accordance with these requirements

c)       Individual location limits equal to or exceeding USD75 million to require STC approval

iii.)         Third Party Custodian Limits

a)       Credit to approve a notional limit for each third-party custodian on which the Bank will be placing reliance reflecting the value of the inventory held

b)       Legal to approve custodian contractual documentation

c)       Credit to specify the insurance cover required and Legal to approve the policy wording for insurance cover against the risk of fraud by the custodian and third parties

d)       Individual third-party custodian limits equal to or exceeding USD75 million to require STC approval

iv.)        Physical Risk Assurance 

a)       Credit to approve procedures for periodic inspection of Locations and Third-Party Custodians 

b)       Inspection procedures to be subject to the following minimum requirements:

i)   inspection team to include a permanent member of the Risk team in the country in which the physical commodity inventory is held ii)      inspections to be performed at least monthly

iii) date of inspection not to be announced more than 2 business days in advance to retain an element of surprise 

ANNEXURE 5  

6.1 Gap Risk Ratings – Definitions

Liquidity Risk is a measure of risk to estimate the time to liquidate a position taken on a bond underlying and it is defined into 5 grades. This measure is now being replaced by a “Gap Risk Rating[9]” measure which better describes the output of the model being used. The use of the Gap Risk Rating remains however unchanged. Moreover, we will continue to define the Gap Risk Rating into 5 grades and will continue to refer to them as L1 to L5 in the systems – they will otherwise be referred to as G1 to G5.

        

 

The following table constitutes the new definitions of each Gap Risk Rating:

Grade 

Description 

G1 

(formerly L1)

Gap Risk is minimal. G1 Assets typically have very low-price volatility, high market liquidity and are generally high-quality assets. These assets are likely to remain stable or appreciate during periods of market stress due to benefiting from “flight to quality” flows

e.g. US Treasury Bills, German Bunds, UK Gilts

G2

(formerly L2)

Highly liquid EM sovereign and investment grade corporates. Low price volatility in normal markets but volatility can increase sharply during periods of market stress. Assets remain liquid however

e.g. SA, Turkey, Russia, Venezuela and Brazil sovereign. Vale, Gazprom, VTB corporates

G3

(formerly L3)

Good liquidity in normal market conditions. Asset volatility and illiquidity likely to increase markedly during periods of market stress. Likely to be larger issue size sub investment grade sovereign and corporate Eurobonds

e.g. Nigeria, Ghana, Ukraine sovereign. GTB, Evraz, Marfrig corporates

G4

(formerly L4)

Restricted liquidity even under normal market conditions. Likely to be smaller and / or lowly rated sub investment grade sovereign and corporate Eurobond issues

e.g. Grenada, Multicanal, Temir

G5

(formerly L5)

Loan Type asset. Very limited (if any) price visibility and secondary market liquidity
