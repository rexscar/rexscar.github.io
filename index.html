Reasons for policy The purpose of this policy is to define the basis upon which functional credit authority is delegated down to committees and individuals employed in the Risk function of Standard Bank Corporate and Investment Banking (“CIB”) globally. Related policies This policy is to be read in conjunction with all other relevant Bank policies in force from time to time relating to the specific division, country, legal entity and business. The Group Credit Standard takes precedence over this policy and in the event of any conflict between this policy and the Group Credit Standard; the Group Credit Standard will prevail. Applicability This policy applies to all CIB functions globally. Sponsor / Owner Sponsor: Chief Risk Officer (“CRO”), CIB Owner: Head: Corporate and Investment Banking Credit Risk Approval: Group Risk Oversight Committee Who should know this policy All those within CIB with responsibility for originating, structuring, recommending, and authorising credit risk. 1. Objectives of Policy 2. The objectives of this Delegated Authority (“DA”) policy are: 2.1 To facilitate and enable CIB to operate as a single integrated business, irrespective of the legal entities into which risk assets may be booked such that the Standard Bank Group (excluding Liberty Holdings) always has one consistent credit view on a client; 2.2 To extend DA to individual Risk Officers on the basis of being personal to the holder in order to increase decision making accountability; 2.3 As far as possible, to streamline the credit approval process in order to increase efficiency and effectiveness of the credit approval process; 2.4 To optimally distribute the sanctioning workload appropriately across CIB between a cadre of experienced Risk Officers, taking cognisance of the quantum and riskiness of specific risk assets, the Bank’s risk appetite, the Bank’s capital base and business volumes; 2.5 To provide for transparent oversight of the decision making of individual holders of DA in order to ensure that the quality of decision making remains consistent and within the risk appetite of the Bank and that the Group Credit Standard is being consistently applied; 2.6 To ensure that any limit to a counterparty or economic group1 is approved at the appropriate level of DA and such limits receives the appropriate degree of attention. The higher the limits and/or the higher the risk profile of the counterparty or economic group, the higher the DA level or approval. Where such approvals are deemed material to the Bank’s performance, they shall be reported to CIB Credit Governance Committee at the discretion of the relevant DA authority; 2.7 To enshrine the ‘four-eyes2’ principle for all credit approvals, while ensuring excesses are approved at appropriate DA level. 2.8 To ensure that the credit view is applied consistently for counterparties or economic groups by ensuring that ownership of any counterparty or economic group resides with a single credit risk team / officer (in line with the Group Credit Manager responsibilities guidelines) regardless of the jurisdictions involved. 1 Economic Group: As defined in the CIB Credit policy, where two or more natural or legal persons who are interlinked and deemed to pose a “single risk”, either by way of control or significant economic interdependence. (refer to the CIB Credit policy for comprehensive definition) 2 4-eye principle is defined as DA holder acting in conjunction with another DA holder. In cases where Credit has agreed that Front-Office teams originate credit applications, and approved by the relevant DA holder, it is deemed that the ‘4eyes’ requirement has been complied with. Should there be a request for any other business unit or legal entity to adopt this business model, approval from the CIB Head, Credit Risk would have to be obtained 3. Functional vs. Legal Entity Credit Approval 3.1 This policy distinguishes between Functional Credit approval and Legal Entity Credit approval. 3.1.1 “Functional Credit Approval” is an assessment of the creditworthiness of a particular credit proposal applying the principles enshrined in the Group Credit Risk Governance Standard and based on the risk assessment of the individual counterparty or economic group. This should take into account, inter alia, industry risk, the country or countries of risk as well as specific product or structural issues and their appropriateness in line with credit policies across all legal entities exposed to that counterparty or economic group. However, this functional approval will not necessarily take into consideration any specific issues which may be relevant to the specific legal entity within Standard Bank Group where any asset thus approved is expected to be booked, such as regulatory or compliance provisions specific to the legal entity as outlined in clause 3.1.2. 3.1.2 “Legal Entity Credit Approval” considers any issues which may arise with respect to the specific legal entity within Standard Bank Group where an asset is expected to be booked. This would include, inter alia, specific legal entity matters related to booking the asset on the specific legal entity’s balance sheet (e.g. liquidity, the requirement for the Board support of the specific legal entity), exchange control, single obligor limit constraints, general regulatory concerns, common directorship conflicts, legal entity concentrations, and local accountability to and responsibility of legal entity stakeholders to ensure that the risk reward dynamics for that legal entity remain appropriate and accepted. 3.2 This policy deals with Functional Credit approval authority in the first instance. Policies dealing with “Legal Entity Credit Approval” authority may be required to be procured within the specific Legal Entity on whose balance sheet the credit exposure will reside as detailed above.
